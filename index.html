Max Magaña

I am an independent political science researcher studying political behavior, social identity, social capital, intergroup conflict, and polarization.

I am currently writing a short book that develops a framework for understanding how parties function as superordinate coalitions in two-party systems, how hierarchy orientation structures partisan sorting, and how narratives help partisans adapt to prolonged political conflict and contribute to polarization.

More broadly, my research interests include political psychology, social psychology, public opinion, intergroup relations, political engagement, and media effects.
